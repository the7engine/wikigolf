About

Origins

The first reference to Wiki Golf is hard to determine. The Urban Dictionary has an entry defining the game from 18th April, 2017. There has been a Facebook group pushing an app version of the game since 22nd August, 2010. But this app isn't available in Australia. The first time I witnessed the game was in 2009 whilst serving in the Royal Australian Air Force at 33 Squadron.

There was a substantial delay in the delivery of the new aircraft platform and as such, the engineers had to find ways to pass the time. Someone suggested visiting a random Wikipedia article, and then provided another verified Wikipedia article. Using only the links provided in the body of the article, one would navigate from one article and land at the other article without using the browser's back function. The player with the least amount of clicks would be crowned the winner.

This developed into the game we know today, with 19 articles chosen at random, and players navigating between each one to earn a score over 18 holes. Without a system to detect usage of the back funtion, players were expected to keep score as they do in the physical game of golf. In a bid to rush the game and finish as soon as possible, players began using the browser's Ctrl + F function. Searching for keywords in the article without having to read the article itself. This is seen as the lazy man's Wiki Golf, and should be discouraged. What's the worst that could happen, you learn something?

Evolution

After sharing the yarn about the origins of the game, and passing the rite of passage on to the next breed of procastinators, it was seen that the game needed a place to live. A domain if you will. Here the games roots and exciting gameplay can be shared and spread across the global community. To get players acquainted we have developed some iterations of the game to get the novice hooked on the sport.

The best place to start, is always the Driving Range. This presents a player with a random artice from Wikipedia. The goal being to navigate to another random article in as few clicks as possible. Once you have honed your skills on the range, you can take it to the Pitch 'n' Putt arena. These are short 9 hole Courses with simple topics to keep strokeplay to a minimum.

Once you have it dialed in, you can have a quick round of 9 holes, or challenge yourself at get through the full 18 holes. There are a number of courses to test yourself on. By all means, create your own and become a Wiki Golf Pro.

Future

While the game is always evolving. The next chapter will be to have some professional scoring, record keeping, umpiring of play and course development. Look out for future improvements as the game grows and becomes more professional than it already is.

Some upcoming improivements are:

  • Prevent browser back function
  • Track the number of clicks made
  • Detect when the hole is complete
  • Create user database to track scores
